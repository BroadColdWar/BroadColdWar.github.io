The Cold War in Berlin

MANIA
Connections

Stalin

Stalin laid the groundwork. The 1948 Berlin Blockade, which cut off land access to West Berlin, was the first real test of wills over the city. It failed, but it established Berlin as the place where Cold War tensions would keep playing out, all the way to 1961.

Gorbachev

Gorbachev did not tear down the Wall, but his glasnost and perestroika policies changed what was politically possible. By 1989, communist governments no longer had Soviet backing to use force against their own populations. Without that backing, the Wall could not stand.

1948 Arab-Israeli War

The direct connection is not obvious, but both events came from the same broken post-WWII order: borders drawn by outside powers, displaced populations, superpowers inserting themselves everywhere. Berlin and the Middle East were two of the first places where that instability became permanent.
