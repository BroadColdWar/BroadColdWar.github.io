Today & Beyond

The Wall Today

Physical borders

The Berlin Wall kept people in. The U.S.-Mexico barrier is argued as keeping people out. The purposes differ, but both became political symbols well beyond their practical function. Since 1990, the number of border walls worldwide has roughly tripled. The Wall is gone. The impulse to build them is not.

Surveillance

The Stasi had 97,000 full-time employees monitoring 17 million East Germans, plus roughly 170,000 civilian informants. A government today with access to smartphones and facial recognition can cover far more ground with far fewer people. What changed is the scale. The logic is the same.

Propaganda

East Germany called its side the anti-fascist state. West Berlin was held up as proof that capitalism worked. Neither side let the facts get in the way of the image. Social media has not changed that dynamic; it has just made it faster and harder to trace back to a single source.

Alliances

NATO outlasted the Wall and is still expanding. When Russia invaded Ukraine in 2022, the Cold War language came back almost verbatim: buffer zones, spheres of influence, Western encirclement. The specific conflict changed. The underlying structure of competing alliances, with smaller states caught in between, did not.
