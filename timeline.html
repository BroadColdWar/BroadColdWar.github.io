Timeline

Key moments

The Berlin Wall did not appear out of nowhere. It grew out of postwar divisions, alliance systems, and repeated crises over Berlin.

1945

Germany and Berlin are divided

After World War II, the Allied powers divide Germany and Berlin into occupation zones. This creates the foundation for a divided Germany.

1948-1949

Berlin Blockade and Airlift

Stalin's Soviet Union blocks land access to West Berlin. The United States and its allies supply the city by air, proving that Berlin would become a major Cold War flashpoint.

1961

The Wall goes up

East Germany closes the border and begins building the Wall to stop people from leaving for the West.

1963

Kennedy visits West Berlin

President John F. Kennedy gives his famous Berlin speech to show American support for West Berlin.

1987

Reagan challenges Gorbachev

Ronald Reagan calls on Soviet leader Mikhail Gorbachev to "tear down this wall," turning the Wall into an even stronger symbol of Soviet control.

1989

The Wall opens

Protests, reform movements, and confusion over travel rules lead East Germans to cross into West Berlin. The fall of the Wall becomes a major turning point near the end of the Cold War.
