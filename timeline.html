Timeline

1945–1989

1945 Germany and Berlin are divided after World War II The Allies divide Germany into four occupation zones. Berlin, inside the Soviet zone, is also split among the four powers. This creates the future conflict over who controls Germany and what political system it follows.
1948–1949 Berlin Blockade and Airlift Stalin's Soviet Union blocks all land routes into West Berlin. The U.S. and its allies respond with a massive airlift, flying in food and supplies for 11 months. It becomes the first major Cold War standoff in Berlin.
1961 The Wall goes up overnight East Germany closes the border on August 13 and begins building barriers. By morning, families are separated, streets are blocked, and the escape route West Berliners used is gone. Construction continues for years.
1963 Kennedy in West Berlin Two years after the Wall goes up, JFK addresses a crowd of roughly 450,000 in West Berlin. His "Ich bin ein Berliner" speech is a direct statement of U.S. solidarity with West Berlin and its most famous Cold War moment.
1987 Reagan at the Brandenburg Gate Ronald Reagan stands at the Brandenburg Gate and calls directly on Gorbachev to tear down the Wall. The speech becomes famous not just for the line, but for linking the Wall's existence to the Soviet bloc's credibility.
1989 The border opens After months of protests and pressure for reform, a government spokesman announces new travel rules at a press conference — and misreads the timing. Crowds flood the checkpoints. Guards stand down. The Wall effectively falls that night.
