Overview

What was the Berlin Wall?

The Berlin Wall was a fortified barrier built by communist East Germany beginning on August 13, 1961. It separated East Berlin from West Berlin and surrounded West Berlin until the border opened on November 9, 1989.

The basic problem

After World War II, Germany was divided into occupation zones controlled by the United States, Britain, France, and the Soviet Union. Berlin was also split into zones, even though the city sat inside Soviet-controlled East Germany.

Why it was built

Millions of people had been leaving East Germany for the West. East Germany built the Wall to stop that escape route and protect the image of the communist system.

The Berlin Wall was more than concrete. It was a public admission that East Germany needed force to keep its own citizens from leaving — making it one of the clearest symbols of the Cold War.
