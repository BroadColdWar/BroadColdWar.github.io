Mission Briefing

This page is where we explain the Berlin Wall clearly before getting into analysis. The goal is not just to say "the Wall divided Berlin," but to show why it became such a strong Cold War symbol.

What was it?

The Berlin Wall was a barrier built by East Germany in 1961 to separate East Berlin from West Berlin. It was supposed to stop people from leaving the communist East for the capitalist West.

Why Berlin?

Berlin was deep inside East Germany, but the city itself was split into occupation zones. That made West Berlin a weird Cold War island surrounded by Soviet-controlled territory.

The Wall was not just concrete. It was proof that the Cold War was also about image, ideology, fear, and controlling people.
