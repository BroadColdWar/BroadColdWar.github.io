Overview

What was the Berlin Wall?

The Berlin Wall was a guarded barrier built by East Germany beginning on August 13, 1961. It separated East Berlin from West Berlin and stood until the border opened on November 9, 1989.

Why Berlin was divided

After World War II, Germany was divided into occupation zones controlled by the United States, Britain, France, and the Soviet Union. Berlin was also divided, even though it was located inside the Soviet zone. This made West Berlin a capitalist city surrounded by communist East Germany.

Why the Wall was built

Before the Wall, many East Germans escaped to the West through Berlin. East Germany built the Wall to stop people from leaving. The government claimed it was protecting socialism, but the Wall also showed that the state needed force to prevent its citizens from escaping.

The basic argument

The Berlin Wall mattered because it made the Cold War visible. Instead of just being a conflict between ideas, speeches, and alliances, the Cold War became something people could physically see: concrete, barbed wire, checkpoints, guards, and separated families.
